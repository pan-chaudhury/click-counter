## Design Context

### Users
Solo user, testing and fidgeting. No accounts, no sharing, just open and click.

### Brand Personality
Playful, bouncy, satisfying. It should feel like a toy you'd fidget with. Three words: playful, tactile, delightful.

### Aesthetic Direction
Toybox aesthetic. Warm light theme with soft pastel surface. Big chunky candy-colored button that begs to be clicked. Large bold counter number with bouncy pop animation on each increment. Rounded, friendly typography. The whole thing should feel like a physical toy, not software.

### Color System
- **Background:** soft warm cream `#FFF5EB`
- **Surface:** warm white with subtle shadow
- **Button:** bright candy coral/orange `#FF6B4A`
- **Button hover:** slightly saturated `#FF5733`
- **Button active/pressed:** darker, scaled down `#E54D2E`
- **Counter text:** warm charcoal `#2D2A26`
- **Reset text:** muted warm gray `#A39E98`
- **Shadows:** warm-tinted, soft

### Design Principles
- The button is the whole interface — make it irresistible to click.
- Every click should feel satisfying: tactile press animation + number bounce.
- Keep it dead simple: one button, one number, one tiny reset link.
- Milestone moments (10, 50, 100) get a subtle celebration.
- Nothing sharp, dark, or serious. This is a toy.
