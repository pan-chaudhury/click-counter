## Design Context

### Users
Solo user, testing and fidgeting. No accounts, no sharing, just open and click.

### Brand Personality
Bubblegum Pop Y2K revival. Unapologetically fun, nostalgic, candy-plastic. Three words: translucent, bubbly, iridescent.

### Aesthetic Direction
Full Y2K commitment. Translucent jelly surfaces, iridescent highlights, soft plastic glow. The whole thing should look like it's made of molded translucent resin. Think iMac G3 meets Polly Pocket meets a Japanese capsule machine. A toy from 2002 you found in a drawer, turned on, and the screen still works.

### Color System
- **Background:** Soft gradient — lavender to baby pink to aqua. Iridescent shimmer.
- **Button:** Translucent jelly — see-through plastic with glossy highlight, pink-tinted
- **Button pressed:** Deeper translucency, squishy press animation
- **Counter text:** Hot pink or bubblegum pink, big and inflated
- **Combo low:** Pastel glow
- **Combo high:** Hot pink → iridescent glow
- **Reset text:** Frosted lilac
- **Shadows:** Colored and soft — pink-tinted, not gray. Deep and diffused like light through plastic.
- **Confetti:** Translucent pastel shapes — circles, stars, hearts

### Typography
Something round, fat, inflated — bubble letters energy. Not Nunito. The counter number should look chunky and bubbly.

### Design Principles
- Full Y2K commitment — no hedging, no restraint, no earthiness
- Everything should look like translucent molded plastic or jelly
- The button is a squishy gummy bear you want to press
- Glossy plastic surfaces with soft inner glow and subtle transparency
- Confetti should feel like candy falling out of a capsule machine
- Combo escalation goes from pastel → hot pink → iridescent
- Nothing warm, earthy, minimal, dark, or techy
